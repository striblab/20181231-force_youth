Decreasing force against black youth
As a percentage of all police use-of-force cases, incidents against black youth aged 16-to-24 and black young adults aged 25-to-34 gradually declined the past decade.

Persistent racial disparities remain

A widespread occurrence
Police use-of-force incidents against black youth and black young adults are most common in North and Central Minneapolis, but also occur across other neighborhoods of the city regardless of crime rate, mapped here per 1,000 residents from 2000 to 2017 and shaded from less to more.
