Police are using force against black youth less often
Police use-of-force incidents where the suspect was a black male aged 16 to 24 has dropped about eight percentage points since 2008.
Source: Minneapolis Police Department | Graphic by Jeff Hargarten, Star Tribune
Police still more often use force against black youth
Black youth alone comprised about a quarter of all police use-of-force incidents – nearly 4,000 records total – from 2008 through 2018.
Source: Minneapolis Police Department | Graphic by Jeff Hargarten, Star Tribune
Spread across the city
Police use-of-force incidents against black youth are most common in North and Central Minneapolis, but still happen all over other areas of the city regardless of crime rate. Mapped against average crime rate per 1,000 residents from 2000 to 2017, shaded from less to more.
Source: Minneapolis Police Department | Graphic by Jeff Hargarten, Star Tribune
